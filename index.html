Computer vision-based road accident detection and reporting system

Description

The rapid increase in usage of vehicles has increased the rate of road accidents. Many drivers unaware of the accident on their route, start to queue up on the lanes. Road accidents can occur for various reasons like drunk drivers, over speeding, road anomalies etc. Instead, if they would have known that there has been an accident on that path, they could then have changed their route to reach their destinations while the emergency help reaches the accident location. So, we are discussing a software system, in which vehicles and surveillance cameras can detect the accident and report it emergency and other drivers who are planning to travel via same location can be intimated. To solve this problem, we will use Computer Vision. We will discuss CNN (computational neural networks) to detect road accidents in this tutorial.

Introduction and Problem Definition:

In case of any accident, the road gets blocked causing traffic jam. This traffic can delay the emergency help from reaching the location as well as delay other drivers on that route. To avoid traffic delays, road accidents need to be reported to emergency as well other riders on that path. Today most of the roads have surveillance cameras installed and many new vehicles have cameras installed on them which record the traffic around them. In this discussion, we will have a look at road accidents caused between two cars and how they can be detected using computer vision.

Sensor Possibilities:

Input to the system can be from grayscale surveillance cameras and car cameras.
